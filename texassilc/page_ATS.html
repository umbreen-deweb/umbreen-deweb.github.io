Job Access Reverse Commute (JARC) Transportation Program

The Job Access Reverse Commute (JARC) Transportation Program developed by the Texas Department of Transportation is a comprehensive approach to providing transportation to transition low-income and welfare reform clients into employment.

The JARC Program ensures:

  • affordable transit for low-income and welfare recipients;
  • personalized services to help individuals join or form carpools and vanpools;
  • develop transportation alternatives that better link low-income individuals to job centers;
  • subsidize leased vans or establish vanpools with community agencies; and
  • partner with employers, jurisdictions, housing authorities and social service agencies on innovative transportation strategies for low-income populations.

Assess & Deliver is the expansion of the scope of the original Transportation Works project. It includes all rural and small urban areas of Texas. Specifically, the Texas SILC will adhere to the Independent Living philosophy by identifying service, infrastructure, and other barriers to the use of existing public transportation in rural and small urban areas by individuals with disabilities. Understanding barriers to the use of accessible public transportation in rural areas will help in addressing the overall employment gap that hinders independence.

The Transportation Works: Assess & Deliver project successfully:

  • Solicited the participation and involvement of transportation partners, passengers, consumers, advocates, human service providers, and other stakeholders through surveys, public hearings, and works groups to identify transit barriers.
  • Published an extensive report on the Transportation Works: Assess & Deliver survey findings. This includes feedback gathered from public hearings and work group discussions. As well as recommendations for traditional and non-traditional forms of public transportation.
  • Presented findings at the statewide Transportation Works Summit in Lubbock, Texas August 16-18, 2015.
  • Educated and provided technical training to CIL leadership and regional transportation providers seeking to implement additional transportation programs or service to improve employment opportunities.

The Texas State Independent Living Council is not an advisory council.

Instead we are a non-profit that focuses on planning and coordinating funding for Independent Living services.
